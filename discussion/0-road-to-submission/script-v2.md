## THE DEMO

### COLD OPEN (10s)

**[Screen: Jarvis app open, Projects tab visible showing "Q4 Strategy Briefing" and "Tech Trends" projects]**

> Riya uses Jarvis to capture knowledge — articles, videos, emails, meetings. She's been at it for weeks. Two projects already in her library.
>
> Then one email changes her day.

---

### ACT 1 — THE EMAIL (20s)

**[Screen: Browser tab → Chrome tabs listed → Gmail tab visible]**

> 8:30 AM. Her VP Vikram sends an email: "Leadership wants to green-light the agentic AI platform. Kickoff call at 4 PM. Pull together what we know."

**[Screen: Click capture on Gmail tab → Gem saved toast → auto-tags: `agentic-ai`, `project-kickoff`]**

> One click. Email captured as a gem. Tagged, summarized, searchable. But it's sitting loose — she needs a project.

---

### ACT 2 — PROJECT CREATION + THE NUDGE (45s)

**[Screen: Projects tab → click "New Project"]**

> She creates a new project: "Agentic AI Platform — Customer Support."

**[Screen: Fill in title, description ("Research and plan an AI agent platform for Tier 1 customer support automation"), objective ("Technical design doc + cost estimate for Q2 prototype")]**

> Title, description, objective — done.

**[Screen: Project created → Jarvis shows a nudge / recommendation panel: "Relevant gems found in your library"]**

> Here's where Jarvis earns its keep. It scans her existing gem library and nudges her — "You already have gems related to this."

**[Screen: Show recommended gems from both old projects with relevance scores]**

> From "Q4 Strategy Briefing" — the strategy call recording where the team first discussed AI agents and support gaps. From "Tech Trends" — the Bedrock deep dive, the Strands SDK article, the piece on agentic AI evolution.

**[Screen: Select gems → click "Add to Project" → gems copied into new project]**

> She picks four gems across both projects. Knowledge she captured weeks ago — now the foundation of today's research. No re-reading. No searching through bookmarks. It's already enriched, tagged, and ready.

**[Screen: Add the VP email gem too → Project now has 5 gems]**

> Add the VP's email. Five gems in the project and she hasn't opened a single new tab yet.

---

### ACT 3 — RESEARCH SPRINT (90s)

**[Screen: Open Chrome → navigate to a Medium article about multi-agent cost analysis]**

> Now she goes hunting. She finds a Medium article on the real cost of running multi-agent systems in production.

**[Screen: Switch to Jarvis → Browser tab → Chrome tabs listed with source types → click capture on Medium article]**

> Switch to Jarvis. Browser tab shows all her Chrome tabs, auto-classified. One click — article extracted, saved as a gem, auto-enriched.

**[Screen: Open another article in Chrome — agent orchestration patterns]**

> Another article — orchestration patterns: chains vs. graphs vs. swarms. Capture.

**[Screen: Capture it in Jarvis → two new gems added]**

> Two articles, two gems. Each one tagged, summarized, added to the project in under 10 seconds.

**[Screen: Open YouTube → AWS Summit video on building production agents]**

> She watches a short YouTube video on building production AI agents. Jarvis's background observer auto-detects it — notification badge appears.

**[Screen: YouTube tab in Jarvis → video detected → click Capture → gem saved]**

> One click. Title, channel, duration, description — extracted and saved.

**[Screen: Open ChatGPT in Chrome → have a conversation about Bedrock vs open-source agents]**

> Now she goes to ChatGPT. She asks it to compare Bedrock AgentCore with open-source alternatives — pricing models, feature gaps, migration paths. A quick back-and-forth to pressure-test her thinking.

**[Screen: Switch to Jarvis → Browser tab → ChatGPT tab detected → click Capture]**

> Back to Jarvis. The Browser tab sees the ChatGPT conversation. One click — the entire exchange extracted and saved as a gem. Her ChatGPT research is now part of her project, not lost in a browser tab.

**[Screen: Also show Claude side panel capture if available — or skip]**

> She could do the same with Claude's side panel — Jarvis captures those too via the Accessibility API.

**[Screen: Project view → show all gems listed — now 9-10 gems total]**

> By noon, Riya has ten gems in her project. Four from past projects she'd forgotten about. The VP's email. Two articles. A YouTube video. A ChatGPT conversation. All searchable, all tagged, all enriched.

---

### ACT 4 — PROJECT SUMMARY (45s)

**[Screen: Project view → Summary tab → click "Generate Summary"]**

> Two hours before the call. Ten gems, but she needs a briefing, not a pile of links.

**[Screen: Spinner → "Analyzing gems..." → Summary appears]**

> Jarvis reads every gem's knowledge file, chunks them, summarizes each chunk, merges into one cohesive overview.

**[Screen: Summary text — key themes, cost analysis, open questions]**

> Key themes across 10 sources. Three sources agree: graph-based orchestration beats chains for customer support. Managed platforms cost 30-40% more but save headcount. The Q4 strategy call already flagged 68% repetitive tickets. Open questions: build vs. buy, latency targets, escalation paths.

**[Screen: Q&A — type "What are the strongest arguments for Bedrock vs building our own?"]**

> She asks questions against the summary. Answers grounded in her actual research — not generic AI knowledge.

**[Screen: Click "Save as Gem" → summary saved]**

> Save as a gem. The synthesis joins the project. A checkpoint she can share with Vikram and revisit later.

---

### ACT 5 — THE CALL (90s)

**[Screen: Record tab → click Start Recording]**

> 4 PM. Riya joins the call. She hits Record.

**[Screen: Transcript tab — words appearing in real-time]**

> Live transcription. Words appear as they speak. All on-device — no audio leaves her machine.

**[Screen: Co-Pilot tab → toggle on → first cards appear]**

> She turns on Co-Pilot. Every 60 seconds, Jarvis feeds raw audio to a multimodal LLM and surfaces insight cards.

**[Screen: Cards sliding in — color-coded]**

> Blue card — key point: "VP confirms $50K budget for Q1 prototype."
>
> Green card — decision: "Use Bedrock AgentCore for V1."
>
> Amber card — action item: "Riya to prepare technical design doc by next Friday."

**[Screen: Red question card]**

> Red card — open question: "Latency requirement not addressed. Suggested: ask about this."
>
> Riya glances at it and asks. Vikram says "under two seconds." A new decision card slides in.

**[Screen: Card stack — multiple cards, some auto-collapsed]**

> 35 minutes. Four decisions, six action items, three key points — captured in real-time. Riya didn't take a single note.

---

### ACT 6 — POST-MEETING (45s)

**[Screen: Stop recording → Session Summary Card appears]**

> Call ends. Session Summary Card — 35-minute kickoff, budget confirmed, Bedrock chosen, design doc due Friday.

**[Screen: Chat tab → "List every commitment I made during this call"]**

> She opens Chat. "List every commitment I made." Jarvis pulls from the transcript — design doc by Friday, evaluate Strands, schedule follow-up with Arun, send synthesis to Vikram by EOD.

**[Screen: "What did we NOT discuss that we should have?"]**

> "What did we not discuss?" Jarvis cross-references with the pre-meeting research — multi-model routing, detailed cost projections, human escalation path, success metrics beyond resolution rate.

**[Screen: Save recording as Gem → added to project]**

> Save as a gem. Transcript, Co-Pilot analysis, all metadata — preserved. It joins the project.

---

### ACT 7 — THE PAYOFF (20s)

**[Screen: Project view → all gems listed]**

> One email at 8:30 AM. Eleven knowledge artifacts by 5 PM.
>
> Gems from old projects she'd forgotten. Articles and videos captured in seconds. A ChatGPT conversation saved, not lost. A research synthesis generated. A meeting with every decision captured.
>
> All searchable. All tagged. All in one project.

---

### CLOSING (15s)

**[Screen: Jarvis app — full view]**

> Other tools transcribe your meetings. Jarvis remembers your entire day.
>
> Built with Tauri and Rust. All AI runs locally. Nothing leaves your machine.
>
> This is Jarvis.
