# Jarvis — Demo Script (Draft)

**Format:** Screen recording with voiceover (~5–6 min)
**Structure:** Follow Riya's story — one day, one email, ten gems

---

## COLD OPEN (15s)

**[Screen: Empty Jarvis app, dark theme]**

> You get an email at 8:30 AM. By 5 PM, you need a project brief, a research stack, and a meeting with decisions captured.
>
> Most people end the day with 23 browser tabs, a half-written doc, and the feeling they forgot something.
>
> Riya ends it with 10 knowledge artifacts, every source preserved, every decision searchable.
>
> This is Jarvis.

---

## ACT 1 — CAPTURE THE EMAIL (30s)

**[Screen: Switch to Browser tab → Chrome tabs listed → Gmail tab visible]**

> Riya's VP sends an email: "Leadership wants to green-light the agentic AI platform. Kickoff call at 4 PM. Pull together what we know."
>
> One click in Jarvis's Browser tab. The email is extracted — subject, body, metadata — and saved as a Gem.

**[Screen: Click capture → Gem saved toast → Show gem in library with auto-generated tags and summary]**

> Jarvis auto-enriches it on save. Tags: `agentic-ai`, `project-kickoff`. Summary: "Green light for agentic AI platform — kickoff at 4 PM."
>
> That email is now searchable. It's not sitting in an inbox — it's part of a knowledge base.

---

## ACT 2 — CREATE A PROJECT (30s)

**[Screen: Click Projects tab → Create Project dialog]**

> Riya creates a project: "Agentic AI Platform Research." Title, description, objective — done.

**[Screen: Project created → Add the email gem to project]**

> She adds the email gem. But here's where it gets interesting — Jarvis already has gems from weeks ago that are relevant.

**[Screen: Search gems → find old YouTube keynote, Medium article, recording transcript → add to project]**

> A YouTube keynote from three weeks ago. A Medium article from two weeks back. A recording from last month's strategy call. Knowledge she'd forgotten about — instantly relevant again.
>
> Three old gems, one new email. The project already has context.

## ACT 3 — RESEARCH SPRINT (90s)

**[Screen: Project Research tab → AI suggests topics]**

> Jarvis's Research Agent looks at the project and suggests search topics: agent orchestration frameworks, tool-use patterns, cost optimization, Bedrock vs. open-source.

**[Screen: Topic list with checkboxes → click "Run Research"]**

> Riya curates the list — removes one, adds a custom topic — then hits "Run Research." Jarvis searches the web via Tavily and her own gem library simultaneously.

**[Screen: Research results appear — web links + gem suggestions with "Add" buttons]**

> Web results she can open in Chrome. Gem suggestions she can add directly to the project.

**[Screen: Switch to Chrome → YouTube video playing]**

> She opens a suggested YouTube video. Jarvis's background observer detects it automatically — notification badge appears on the YouTube tab.

**[Screen: Switch to Jarvis → YouTube tab → video detected → click Capture]**

> One click. Title, channel, duration, description, transcript — extracted and saved as a Gem. Auto-tagged. Auto-summarized.

**[Screen: Browser tab → see Chrome tabs listed → click on Medium article → capture]**

> She opens a Medium article. Browser tab shows all her Chrome tabs with source types auto-classified. One click — article extracted via Readability, saved as a Gem.

**[Screen: Gems library → show 8 gems with tags and summaries]**

> By noon, Riya has 8 gems in her project. No copy-pasting. No Notion. No bookmarks that go stale. Every source enriched with AI tags and summaries. All full-text searchable.

## ACT 4 — PROJECT SUMMARY (45s)

**[Screen: Project view → Summary tab → click "Generate Summary"]**

> Two hours before the call. Eight gems, but she needs a briefing, not a pile of links.

**[Screen: Spinner → "Analyzing gems..." → Summary appears]**

> Jarvis reads every gem's knowledge file, chunks them respecting gem boundaries, summarizes each chunk, then merges into one cohesive overview.

**[Screen: Summary text visible — key themes, contradictions, open questions]**

> Key themes across 8 sources. Three sources agree agent orchestration is moving from chains to graphs. Cost per call is the number one concern. Open questions identified: build vs. buy, single vs. multi-model routing.

**[Screen: Type a question in the summary Q&A input]**

> She can ask questions about the summary — "What are the strongest arguments for Bedrock AgentCore vs. building our own?" — and get answers grounded in her actual research, not generic AI knowledge.

**[Screen: Click "Save as Gem" → summary saved as a new gem]**

> Save the summary as a gem. It joins the project. Now searchable, reusable, a checkpoint she can revisit.

## ACT 5 — THE CALL (90s)

**[Screen: Record tab → click Start Recording]**

> 4 PM. Riya joins the call. She hits Record.

**[Screen: Transcript tab — words appearing in real-time]**

> Live transcription. Words appear as they speak. Three engines available — whisper-rs, WhisperKit, or MLX Omni — all running locally. No audio leaves her machine.

**[Screen: Click Co-Pilot tab → toggle on → cards start appearing]**

> She turns on the Co-Pilot. Every 60 seconds, Jarvis feeds raw audio to a multimodal LLM and surfaces insight cards.

**[Screen: Cards sliding in with animation — color-coded]**

> A blue key point: "VP confirms $50K budget for Q1 prototype."
>
> A green decision: "Team will use Bedrock AgentCore for first iteration."
>
> An amber action item: "Riya to prepare technical design doc by next Friday."

**[Screen: Red question card appears]**

> A red question: "No one addressed — what's the latency requirement? Suggested: ask about this."
>
> Riya glances at it and asks. The VP says "under 2 seconds."

**[Screen: New green decision card slides in]**

> New card: "Decision — latency target under 2 seconds for first response." Captured in real-time.

**[Screen: Show card stack — multiple cards, some collapsed, hover to expand]**

> Cards auto-collapse after a few seconds so they don't distract. Hover to pause. Color-coded by type. The Co-Pilot captured 4 decisions, 6 action items, 3 key points — and Riya didn't take a single note.

## Production Notes

- **Screen recording**: OBS or macOS screen recorder, 1920x1080
- **Voiceover**: Record separately for clean audio, overlay on screen capture
- **Pacing**: Let the UI breathe — don't rush clicks. Pause 1–2s after each action so the viewer sees the result
- **Music**: Subtle ambient/electronic underscore, fade under voiceover
- **Cuts**: Jump-cut between acts (no need to show real-time waiting). Each act is a self-contained scene
- **Pre-load data**: Have gems, projects, and recordings pre-loaded so the demo flows without waiting for real AI processing
- **Fallback**: If live AI enrichment is slow, pre-record those segments and splice in
